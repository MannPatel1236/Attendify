Visual DNA

Surfaces: Deep midnight (#06060A → #1A1F3A radial). Glass-morphism cards (rgba + backdrop-blur). 1px translucent borders.

Color: Signature brand gradient (indigo → teal → green). Used as identity, sparingly. Atmospheric glows in background.

Type: Inter (slightly tighter tracking for headings). Wordmark in gradient. JetBrains Mono for numbers.

Geometry: 14–18px corner radius. Generous padding. Soft glows replace shadows. Cards feel like "floating glass."

Motion: 280ms with subtle ease-out. Atmospheric elements drift slowly in background. Cards lift on hover.

Iconography: Geometric, slightly thicker stroke. Custom icon set feel. Soft glows on primary icons.

Direction B · Aurora Vercel

The distinctive choice. Most "designed" feel. Brand-forward, memorable, modern.

Pros

  • Most distinctive — strong brand identity
  • Premium SaaS marketing site energy
  • Gradient personality makes it memorable
  • Glass-morphism is a refined evolution of clay
  • Works beautifully on large screens (web/desktop)

Cons

  • More visual surface = more places to fail
  • Backdrop-blur is performance-heavy on low-end Android
  • Gradients can feel dated in 2-3 years
  • Risk of looking "too designed" for a productivity tool
