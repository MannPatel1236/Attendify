Visual DNA

Surfaces: Near-black backgrounds (#0A0E14), #11151C cards, 1px subtle borders (#1E2330), zero drop shadows.

Color: One signature accent (Linear's #5E6AD2 electric indigo). Used sparingly. Status colors (green/amber/red) only for semantic state.

Type: Inter for everything, JetBrains Mono for codes, IDs, percentages, and tabular data.

Geometry: 8–12px corner radius. Tight 1px borders. Generous 16–24px internal padding. No rounded buttons, no soft edges.

Motion: 120ms. Snappy, not luxurious. "Engineered" feel over "designed" feel.

Iconography: Geometric, line-based. No emoji. No rounded blobs.

The mature, professional choice. Instant credibility. Zero aesthetic risk. Mature pattern language.

Pros

  • Instantly recognizable as "premium product"
  • High information density without feeling cluttered
  • Strong typography hierarchy does the heavy lifting
  • Mobile-native pattern (works on small screens)
  • Matches what users already know from Linear/Vercel

Cons

  • Less distinctive — could be any "premium SaaS"
  • Risk of feeling "cold" or "corporate"
  • Hard to stand out in app store screenshots
  • Linear clones can feel derivative
